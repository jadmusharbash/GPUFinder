Here are some fun facts about the Geforce 256.

  • The first model in Nvidia's "GeForce" product series was the GeForce 256.
  • It was the first completely Direct3D 7-compliant 3D accelerator and provided a noticeably significant improvement in 3D PC gaming performance.
  • TSMC produced the chip using its 220 nm CMOS technology.
  • The GeForce 256 comes in two versions, each with a distinct kind of SDRAM memory: the SDR version, which was released in October 1999, and the DDR version, which was released in the middle of December 1999.
  • The earlier DDR variant uses DDR SDRAM memory from SK Hynix, whereas the SDR version uses SDR SDRAM memory from Samsung Electronics.
